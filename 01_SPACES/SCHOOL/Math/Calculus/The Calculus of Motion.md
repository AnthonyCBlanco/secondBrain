### Idea
A common use of vector-valued functions is to describe the motion of an object in the plane or in space. A position function $\vec{r}(t)$ gives the position of an object at time $t$. 

In Calc 1 you learn that the derivative of a position function gives us our velocity functions. And then the derivative of our velocity function yields our functions of acceleration of some object at time $t$.
